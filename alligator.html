American Alligator

American Alligators were on the extinction list in 1967, but 20 years later the population increased and they were taken off the list.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Alligator Mississippiensis
  • Type: Reptile
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Life Span: 35 to 50 Years
  • Size: 10 to 15 feet
  • Weight: 1,000 pounds

American Alligators live in the wild in southeastern United States. They are extremely common in Florida and Louisiana. They are excellent swimmers, but clumsy when on land. They can be found in marshes, swamps, bayous, rivers, lakes and ponds. The main threat to American Alligators is caused by human development of the wetlands.
